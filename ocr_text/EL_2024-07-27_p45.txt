lives.62 Their times at Harvard nearly overlapped: Longfellow became a professor at

Harvard in 1836, two years after Sumner graduated. By 1869, Lewis completed the

marble bust of Longfellow, and had photographs of it sent to America. Harvard

affiliates saw the bust and thought favorably of it. According to a July 1869 report of

the Christian Recorder, Lewis, with the help of her contacts in the United States,

solicited funds for the sculpture to be shipped to Harvard. They started a subscription

to raise the $700 (over $15,000 today), and Lewis promised any society who could

raise $100 a terra cotta copy of the bust.63 By 1871, the bust was at Harvard, and

remains there today. Another interaction that placed Lewis and Sumner within one
