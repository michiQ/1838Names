it is not certain whether Lewis spoke to Croly himself, it is likely that Lewis was

aware of the political leanings of the paper. She used the interview to position herself

as a professional, competent, and well-educated woman.311 Perhaps, doing this

interview was her way of making a political statement, of using her very existence to

negate the stereotypes that continued to work against Black progress. As underscored

in chapter one, she understood the power of print media, and her use of this form of

publicity demonstrated her business savvy, tenacity, and intentionality. When asked

about her plans, she made the following comment:

       I intend to make a beautiful statue, as beautiful as I can, and send it to
       the Philadelphia Exposition anonymously. I do this in order that it may
       be judged fairly, and without favor or prejudice. If it is a success, so
       much the better for me, when I claim my work before the world. If it is
       not a success, I shall bow before the public verdict.

Lewis had made it clear that she wanted a fair chance, to have the opportunity to be

judged without bias. Perhaps she chose to make this announcement in this particular

newspaper to position herself as an ambitious professional to a readership who would

be prejudiced against her. If Lewis’ work was judged fairly, it would work against the

racist logic that nonwhite people are incapable of cultural attainments, as defined

within a Eurocentric society.
