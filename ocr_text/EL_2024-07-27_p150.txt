accommodations by that point, it was clear to her and to any nonwhite readers why she

would have had to stay in a “fourth-rate hotel.”292 While the interviewers’ flourish and

ignorance is distracting, what is noted is that Lewis maintained “ease and lady-like

bearing” even while being gawked at, and likely obliged to stay at a “fourth rate hotel”

the interviewer’s description gives us yet another glance at Lewis’ disposition, and

how she might have responded second-class travel conditions, and treatment as a

second class citizen. What the St. Louis Globe and Sun correspondents reveal is that

white America could be surprised by the determination of a woman like Edmonia

Lewis, even while maintaining the very structures that made her experience in

America, even as a visitor, so challenging.
