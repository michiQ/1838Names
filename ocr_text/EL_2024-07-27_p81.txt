woman of color living in Edmonia Lewis’ context, competence may prove to be a

someone who did not believe the “Death of Cleopatra” was her own work.”144

Moreover, in psychology, “playing dumb” is a form of impression management that

has been attributed to women.145 For women, such behavior works to either gain favor

or to prevent ostracism. For marginalized ethnic groups, playing dumb is one of many

strategies used to navigate a multi-ethnic society.

dictated her choices. She knew she was writing mostly for a white audience, many of

whom would read her narrative through the lens of their own preconceived notions

about what it meant to be Native American, and what it meant to be an “Indian girl.”
