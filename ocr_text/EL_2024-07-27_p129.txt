wagon or twenty-five days by stagecoach.248 Not only was this a physically

exhausting trip, but for Black travelers, the mental and emotional strain of navigating

inhospitable, and potentially dangerous conditions might have been overwhelming.

However, as Shirley Ann Wilson Moore explores in her landmark book, Sweet

Freedom's Plains: African Americans on the Overland Trails, 1841–1869, like

Samuel, hundreds of African Americans were successful in making this arduous

journey.249 By the 1870s, it would have cost approximately $50, or about $1,160 in

2024, to travel from New York to San Francisco by sea.250 Travel by railroad cost

between $65 and $140 (between $1500 and $2,240 in 2024). In 1873, the year Lewis

went to San Francisco, it took approximately one week to make the 3,200-mile trip.251

Additionally, by this point, most trains had a ladies car available for the comfort and

safety of (white) women travelers. While still arduous compared to a flight from New

York to San Francisco today, these advancements made travel as a single woman just

that much more accessible.
