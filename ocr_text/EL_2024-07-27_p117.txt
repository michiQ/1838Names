How did her experience of teaching newly emancipated African Americans reflect,

and later add to, her perspective on race, and education? When Lewis made her first

return trip to the United States, in 1869, she attended the Festival of the Teachers of

the Freedman’s Union Commission, where a subscription of $150 was received for a

school for African Americans in Charleston (most likely South Carolina).227 Ada

Howard might have attended the festival. Lewis’ attendance suggests she formed

community while teaching and had ongoing interest in education for African

Americans. If we give more meaning to the experiences Edmonia Lewis had, and the

friends she might have made, we can add more nuance to who she was as a person. In

this case, we might even be able to call her something of a scholar activist, as she

continued to use her intellect and educational background in the production of her

sculptors.
