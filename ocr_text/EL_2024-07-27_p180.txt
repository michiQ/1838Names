Arkansas national conference declared the A.M.E Church “the noblest represented

body of the Negro race on this continent;” and began fundraising for a monument to

Richard Allen at Fairmont Park in Philadelphia, to be unveiled on July 4, of 1876.

Scholars believed that they originally commissioned Lewis to do this bronze bust. The

Reconstruction congress contributed $3000 for the making of this bust, and they asked

for other conferences to support them in this initiative.337 Wilberforce University, via

Bishop Payne, also led in the fundraising for the Allen Monument. The Christian

Recorder published a report on the meeting of the Trustee Board of Wilberforce, in

which it was decided that the 2nd Sunday of September of 1874 would be “set apart

for the Centennial contribution from all the Churches in the Connection.”338
