the Christian Recorder, making it a hub for A.M.E. Church members, affiliates, and

African Americans in general. Bishop Arnett, as well as a few reporters stayed with

her. Edmonia Lewis stayed with Mrs. Sarah Young who owned a boarding house on

1836 Lombard St. While boarding with other African Americans, Lewis likely had the

opportunity to network with other visitors, share her experiences, and make new

friends.294

        Given that Lewis was traveling to America once a year, and at some points,

twice, it is especially important that what she experienced while in America is given

the same attention as who she interacted with in Europe. She stayed in the homes of

Black leaders, Black boarding houses, and was celebrated by Black churches and

clubs. By looking at Lewis experience as a traveler, it is clear that there was a network

of individuals who would open their homes, and safe spaces for African Americans to

rest at night, years before the Green Book was in circulation.
