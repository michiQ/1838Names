This story circulated in newspapers and other print media for many years after this

pamphlet was printed. On September 6, of 1873, a correspondent of the New York Sun

called on Edmonia at the San Francisco hotel in which she was staying while

exhibiting her works there. He titled his interview: “The Colored Sculptress. Inspired

by a Statue of Ben Franklin.” In this interview, Lewis described the moment that

inspired her to become an artist as follows:

        I then went to Boston, thinking I would study music, but, walking
        around the streets, I saw the statue of Benjamin Franklin. I went in a
        different direction, but always came back to the same place. I then went
        to Lloyd Garrison; said I had seen a man standing on a stone, not
        knowing how to describe it differently. He told me what it was . . .130

This anecdote is consistent with what Lewis/the author wrote in How Edmonia Lewis

Became an Artist. What is noteworthy here is that the correspondent pulls from this

part of the interview to create the tagline for the title of the interview, “Inspired by a

Statue of Ben Franklin.” By marking this encounter as the start of her career, Edmonia

makes her story quintessentially American.
