        The plight of the orphan was addressed outside of the genre of fiction as well.

Social reformers would often publish written works addressing the conditions of

orphans as a societal problem. Institutions for ‘paupers,’ ‘waifs,’ and other under

protected groups were established during this period, and the phrases such as

workhouse, orphanage, and “orphan asylum” entered the public lexicon. Proponents of

Victorian ideals, including women’s groups and Christian organizations, publicized

their views on the plight of orphaned children and how society ought to respond.118

Organizations and government institutions were concerned about Indigenous orphans

as well.119 The solution was a process of cultural genocide that unfolded throughout

the nineteenth century. In 1826, six years before Samuel Lewis’ birth, the United
