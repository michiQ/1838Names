Unfortunately, this ruling would do little to benefit Black travelers, especially after the

end of Reconstruction. It is likely then, that Lewis either rode second class, which

offered upholstered seats, or third class, in which passengers had to sit close together

on wooden benches and bring their own food. The odor, filth, and cramped conditions

of these cars often made such a long journey extremely difficult. One can imagine that

Lewis, and others who could afford to take a respite, would have to stop at the hotels

and boarding houses that were being constructed along the train routes in order to get

sufficient rest.
