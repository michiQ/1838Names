       Thus, although Lewis’ pamphlet was not illustrated, its emphasis on her

parent-lessness and her size—“little orphan” and “little friend”—draws on these visual

tropes to engender sympathy in the reader. Those phrases tap into a wide range of

literature and imagery that most readers would have been exposed to directly or

indirectly. Indeed, Frank Leslie’s profile on Lewis starts with the sentence, “Edmonia

Lewis is a little American girl, [emphasis mine] scarcely 22 years of age…”137 Yet,
