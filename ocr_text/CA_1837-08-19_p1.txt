Ministerial Neutrality.

 

Mr. Editor,—Why is it that so many pro-
fessed ministers of the gospel are now en-
deavoring to stand on neutral ground with
regard to moral action in the anti-slavery en-
terprise 2. ‘The time has arrived when but
few clergymen, comparatively, openly oppose
the progress of abolition principles. “Rut al-
though litte or nv opposition 1s manifested,
aud many of ther admit that ours is the
ease of justice, of humanity, and of God.—
they endeavor to exeuse themselves from on
gaging in the important work, as-erting that
they do not feel it to be their duty to act x—
while at the same time they adtit it may be
the duty of others—wish success to the cause
—would by no means stand in the way of
others, but desire to be excused themselves.

Now this mode of reasoning appears to me
to be very inconsistent, especially in mimis-
ters of the gospel. Hit is the duty of any, is
it not of ministers, to “open their mouths
for the dumb,”—to “cry aloud and spare
not, but lift up their voice like a trumpet, and
show the people their trausgressions,’—to
plead for the oppressed, and declare the an-

which rests upon them—that they would con-
sider the awful consequences involved in the
course of conduct they pursue! They are
capable of arousing the whole church into
action ; and the church, with the blessing of
God, can regenerate the world. Is it not
then highly criminal for them to stand aloof
from this important, this momentous work 2
[tis their province—it is their duty to wage
immediate war against every sin ; and think
ye they will stand acquitted at the bar of God,
when the tears, the groans, the stripes, and
the untold woes of suffering millions, shall
there rise up in judgement against them? Is
there no siz in thus standing idle? Are not
the groans and tears of the oppressed, con-
stantly rising to heaven, as so many wit-
nesses against the ‘dumb shepherds? who
stind aloof from duty, and by their example,
cry © peace, peace? when destruction tv our
country, on account of its wickedness, appears
to be rapidly approaching ?_ O that minis-
ters—that christians, would lay these things
to heart—act honestly—remember the golden
tule, “ Do unto others as ye would others
should do unto you ;” they would our light
break forth speedily, and the work of our
hands would be established—* for the mouth
of the Lord hath spoken it.” T.
