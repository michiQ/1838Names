The fifth resolution was for the Centennial committee to “organize a Centennial

Association, or committee, among our ladies, to raise money to carry out the above

recommendation." When it came time to accept the resolutions related to Lewis,

Bishop Arnett spoke in favor of it. He was especially in support of “colored women

being represented in that Exposition.” The resolution was agreed to without debate.

       This unanimous acceptance of this resolution was the culmination of advocacy
on the part of Edmonia Lewis’ close associates, like Douglass and Langston. The

ultimate success in bringing one of Lewis’ sculptures to the Centennial Exposition

would represent a joint effort between Black men and women. It was a committee

appointed by the “Editorial Convention at Cincinnati for the State of Indiana,”

apparently made up of A.M.E. church leaders, who would raise the funds for the

“purpose of aiding the Centennial enterprise by encouraging Miss Edmonia Lewis in

her artwork.” The writer put his faith in the committee, the colored men and women of
