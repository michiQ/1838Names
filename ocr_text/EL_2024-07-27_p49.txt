       Native Americans were depicted in a variety of ways in popular visual culture,

from explicitly racist caricatures to depictions of the “vanishing race.” In addition,

idyllic scenes from Native American life were popular subject matter in Romantic and

Naturalist paintings.74 Lewis must have developed a heightened awareness of

American literary and visual production and surrounding society’s perception of

Native Americans. She had more education than many women her age and spent time

around American and European artists and writers. It would follow that Lewis would

be cognizant of the reaction that aligning her own narrative to that of indigenous

people in popular imagination might solicit.75 In the introduction to her book, Black
