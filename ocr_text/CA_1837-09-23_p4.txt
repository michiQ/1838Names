Lasr Evening, we had the high gratification of
hearing our young and learned friend, Dr. Javes
M'Cuse Sutn, deliver a lecture of an hour's
length, in opposition to the pretensions of Phre-
nologists. And whatever be the supposed facts
upon which these high pretensions (we are not
yet prepared to say principles) of the system are
founded, we must ‘say our friend completely up-
rooted them all. We will not accuse Phre-
nologists of legendry, but certainly there are no
principles in philosuphy, reason, nor revelation,
upon which they can found their theory—it is ba-
sed on assumptions, Their pretensions, to us,
savour too much of witchcraft, to have in them
any reality.

Our friend had a difficult task. He had not
merely defective principles to combat, but fic-
titious pretensions, which have no tangibility to
tuke hold of. We furthermore had the high
scrought superstition of his audience against him;
yet with this fearful- odds, as far as reason and
philosophy weigh any thing, he carried strong
convictions, and made conclusive demunstra-
tions.

We are not, as an individual, concerned about
the trath or fallacy of Phrenology, but in its infin-
ence upon an unthinking community we feel in-
terested. We think it a system tending to infidel-
ity and ruin. If God has given a man the Bump-
ification of Desiructiveness, he ust be destructive
and wicked, accarding to the system: and if on
the other hand, he have the contrary Bumpifica-
tion, of necessity he must be the contrary—other-
wise there ia no reality in Phreno'ogy.

However unpopular the side of the question
taken by our young friend, he sustained himself
with honor to his talent and acquirements. Our
soul feasted upon the exhibition of thought and
learning ; his chaste language and discriminating
arguments would have done credit to three times
his number ef years. We commend the whole
history of Dr. Suith’s life to the colored youth of
our country. At the age of 23, we find him in
possession of a good mechanical trade, with all
the honors of one of the first Literary Institutions
of Enrope, and an honorable profession, in which,
hy judgment and skill, he has already proved
himself eminent.

The whole secret of Dr. Smith's success, tinder
Gop, has consisted in his good character and per-
severing industry. Never did he waste his hours
in idleness, nor his thanghts in pursnit of vanity
but his strong mind and industrious hinds were
early devoted to such pursuits as honor Gon, and
adorn humanity. His whole life has commended
him to the favor of Gop and of man, in which
favor he has progressed and prospered. Children
and youth, follow his example.

We humbly hope our friends and brethren will
give their patronage to Dr. Smith. But give him
a chance, and with good health, under Gop, he
will make the Moral, Literary, aud Physiological
world aL 1s own !

Right of Suffrage:

An effort is now being made, and we believe
with a commendable zeal, by Messrs. Ray and
Bell, the proprietor and agent of this paper, to
get up suitable petitions to the next legislature of
this State, praying for the enfranchisement of
every colored citizen. This, brethren, isan effort
worthy of us. ‘The right of suffrage is ours by
BIRTH RIGIIT, and thongh it be fur a time
ILLEGALLY denied us, it should never be yield-
ed up as lost, so long as Gud is righteous, and man
accountable,

Our white brethren cannot much longer, sus-
tain their present position, in reference tu the
rights of colored citizens. They will soon be
tired of the place of tyrants. That love of liberty
which is daily and hourly growing in their hearts,
will soon act itself out, unicersally and gloriously.
It remains only for us to be virtuous and intelli-
fent—to make ourselves worthy, and we shall
possess the desired boun.

Our inducements to virtue, to industry, to en-
terprize and to all that is intelligent and holy, are
abundantly orer and abore those possessed by our
fathers. If swe be and chourd xot MORE ABUN-
DANTLY than they, in untiting efforts and noble
aspirations, we shall be unworthy the parents
who gave us birth, the age in which we live, and
the nation to which we belong. To live content,
brethren, in 2 Republican State, without the right
of suffrage, is pusillanimously to be MERE AN-
{MALS, AND NOT MEN. It is to EFPACE
from ourselves the image of God, and disgrace
the character of man.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

We hope, readers, no means will be unoccupi.
ed, no expense nor efforts spared, in pu-hing
legal measures in the cause of human nights to
a glorious consummation. Every brother should
willingly, AND AT ONCE, give his one or five
dollars, a« may be found necessary, in sending
our agents to every part of the sta’c. The bre-
thren and their friends, in all the remote tawns,
must be visited. ‘They are alive tu the work, and
only need to be called into action. Righteous-
nesa, reason, the genius of our government, and
what is Infinitely more still, God, are all on our
side. We bave but to ask and receive.

To proscribe any portion of a nation, is to de-
grade that portion. This our citizens have al-
ready learned, and init they no longer have plea,
sure. Bnt contrarywise, it is their interest, OF

every human being in the commonwealth, and in
