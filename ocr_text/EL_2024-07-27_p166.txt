        While it is not possible to pinpoint the date of this meeting, some contextual

information allows us to approximate. Douglass does not explicitly say that this

conversation was their first interaction, which suggests that they could have met prior

to 1863. When Douglass visited New York Central College in 1858 to give his “Self

Made Man” speech, Lewis was around 14 years old, and just about to start her summer

semester there. There were only a few Black students at the school, so she would have

stood out to him.316 Douglass passed through Ohio several times during Lewis’ time as

a student at Oberlin College from 1860 to 1863. In January of 1863, Douglass and

Langston spoke at the National Hall in Cleveland, Ohio in celebration of the

Emancipation Proclamation. Around this time, Douglass and Langston started a

speaking tour across the North and Midwest to recruit African Americans to join the

Union Army. Douglass might not have visited Lewis in the season of spring, as he

suggests in his article, but at the start of the spring semester, sometime in January or

February. Douglass ended publication of his paper, Douglass’ Monthly in 1863,

sometime after this meeting with Lewis. However, there is no direct mention of her in
