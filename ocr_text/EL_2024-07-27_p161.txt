herself in a variety of public spaces across the United States, some accepting, some

prejudiced. Her presentation to Reverend Grimes would be one of several times that

Lewis would bring her art to the Black church. Through Edmonia Lewis’ career in the

United States, we can see the role of the Black Church in supporting the development

of a tradition of African American Arts, and the pivotal role that women played in this

tradition.

        During her fourth trip to the United States in the summer of 1873, Lewis took

several important steps towards making her submission to the Centennial Exhibition in

1876. Lewis had business in several major cities, and her first stop was New York.

She delivered her bust of John Brown, commissioned by the Union League Club, the
progenitor of the Metropolitan Museum of Art. She also gave interviews. Lewis

visited the offices of a relatively new periodical, Daily Graphic: An Illustrated

Evening Newspaper on July 10, 1873. Doing an interview with this paper was a

peculiar, but significant choice. The Daily Graphic’s editor, David Croly, was an

opponent of the abolition movement and the Lincoln white house. In 1863, he
