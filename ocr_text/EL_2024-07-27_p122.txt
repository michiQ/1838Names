       This chapter also speaks to the impact of travel segregation on the experience

of African Americans like Edmonia Lewis. In her 2011 book, The Warmth of Other

Suns: The Epic Story of America’s Great Migration, Isabel Wilkerson defines Jim

Crow as, “a nineteenth-century minstrel figure that would become shorthand for the

violently enforced codes of the southern caste system. The Jim Crow regime persisted

from the 1880s to the 1960s.”233 However, the experience of Edmonia Lewis, who

spent time in Virginia, but mostly traveled through the North and West during

Reconstruction, calls attention to the experiences of a smaller, yet still significant part

of the Black population. There are tangentially related essays, such as Myra B. Young

Armstead’s 2005 essay, “Revisiting Hotels and Other Lodgings: American Tourist

Spaces through the Lens of Black Pleasure-Travelers, 1880-1950,” that offer insight

on the experiences of people in the same social class as Lewis.234 However, most
