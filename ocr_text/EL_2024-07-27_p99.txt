15 years old. By that point, her education surpassed that of most girls her age of any

race. Her academic performance was excellent, and her score in conduct would have

made it easy for someone like Reverend John Keep, Oberlin College Trustee, and his

wife Lydia, to take her into their home. As an educated, nonwhite girl, she would have

stood out, and caught the attention any of New York Central College’s sponsors,

abovementioned. Perhaps one of them assisted in helping her transition to Oberlin

College, where she would continue to excel.


                         Edmonia Lewis at Oberlin College

       In 1859, Edmonia Lewis enrolled in the Young Ladies’ Preparatory

Department at Oberlin College in Oberlin, Ohio. Oberlin had been open to African

American students for just over twenty years, partially due to the efforts of Oberlin

Trustee, John Keep.185 Reverend Keep was a highly influential leader at Oberlin

College. He fundraised for the college and cast the vote that allowed for the entry of

black students. He was an elderly man by the time Edmonia Lewis was living in his

home.186 Oberlin College boasted its largest attendance in 1860.187 Around the time

that Lewis came to Oberlin, the town had about 3,000 residents and 800 students.188
