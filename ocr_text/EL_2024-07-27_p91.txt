               New York Central College: A Fundamental Education

       In an interview with The Daily Graphic, Edmonia Lewis said, “Until I was

twelve years old I led this wandering life . . . I was then sent to school for three years

in McGraw . . . From this school I was sent to another, at Oberlin, in Ohio, where I

remained four years.” Edmonia Lewis, 1866.168 When Lewis began her education, she

was about 12 or 13 years old. McGraw was twenty-five miles east of Ithaca, in the

village of McGraw, near Cortland, New York. McGrawville, the school, later known

as New York Central College, was founded by the American Free Baptist Mission

Society in 1848. The college was based on the principles of the society, it was a

racially integrated manual labor school with a commitment to abolitionism.169 As a

school founded on equal rights and Christian reform, Lewis was taught the morals and

habits that would prepare her for Oberlin, and later, for society amongst the middle

and upper classes of America and Europe. Furthermore, New York Central College

offered “rhetorical classes with daily exercises and extemporaneous speaking,” which
