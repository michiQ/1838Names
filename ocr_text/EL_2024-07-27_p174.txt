        By organizing themselves, Black newspapers owners could exercise more

control over their ability to interpret their past and present, and to anticipate the future.

To these convention participants, commemoration and historical interpretation,

motivated by both history and futurity, were interwoven projects that had to be

addressed simultaneously. Emancipation, the Centennial, and anticipation of

Centennials to come, made historiography and commemoration particularly urgent

priorities. Edmonia Lewis’ position as part of a class of aspiring professionals

coincided with the organization of the Black press. With a stronger media network,

Lewis would be able to receive media coverage within her most earnest audience, and

she would be included in larger socio-political conversations about cultural production

and racial uplift.
