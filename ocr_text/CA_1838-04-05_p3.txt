RETROGRADE STEP.

When Pennsylvania in 1790 formed her present Cun-
stitution—surrounded as she then was on all sides with
slaveholding states, for not only Maryland and Dela-
ware, but New-Jersey and New-York, were then slave-
holding states—the article establishing and defining the
tight of suffrage, which, ‘as reported, limited the right to
free white citizens, was,on motion of Mr. Gallatin, then
amember of the Convention, amended by striking out |
the word white—thus leaving tu freemen, of whatever j
color, the main right of a freeman, that of voting.

In the year 1838—and con-equently after the lapse of
nearly half a century, during which, it is boasted, the
mind of man has made great progress—The state of
Pennsylvania is again assembled in convention to revise
its Constitution; and on re-examining and resetiling
this right of suffrage, a Mr. Martin proposes, and the
state of Pennsylvania asembled in convention, after
elaborate debate, accepts an amendment by which the
word “white” is introduced into the clause, so as to
confine the right of voting to free white men! The
vote was 77 for, 45 against thé amendment.

And during this same period, New-York and New-
Jersey have ceased entirely to permit slavery within
their borders, and Delaware is hardly a slave holding
state any longer--and Maryland not by any means to
such an extent as she was. .
