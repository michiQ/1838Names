was the fact that Edmonia Lewis did not have the same class privileges and

conveniences as the white women artists that journalists and historians have associated

her with. In Rome, she did not have access to their social circles without an

interlocuter, and in America she did not have access to the same accommodations as

her female counterparts. Moreover, harsh white critics did not grant her the credibility

they granted to her white contemporaries, which affected every aspect of her career.

To prove herself to doubtful white critics, she personally undertook the manual labor

associated with making marble sculptures for several years, whereas other sculptors

had a team of employees. Despite that fact, she still encountered people who did not

believe her work was her own, even after gaining recognition on both sides of the

Atlantic. This unfair treatment extended to her experience of completing business

transactions as well, including the process of shipping her works. For this reason, it is

important to grasp what Lewis’ experience of travel might have been like to genuinely

appreciate the magnitude of her accomplishments.
