prior to coming to Boston. This chapter is also in conversation with Kirsten Pai

Buick’s essay, “Lifting as She Climbed: Mary Edmonia Lewis, Representing and

Representative,” published in the anthology, Women Artists of the Harlem

Renaissance.166 Buick reveals that Lewis spent a short period of her early childhood in

Newark, New Jersey with her father’s family and then in Canada and the Finger Lakes

region with her mother’s family.167 She pursued her formal education in Central New

York and Northern Ohio. Her acculturation in these environments helped her

transition to Boston, where she started her career. She was an artist whose sculptures
