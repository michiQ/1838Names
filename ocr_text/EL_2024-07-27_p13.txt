Lewis was indeed one such woman. Given the extent to which Lewis’ art influenced

and was influenced by the literary and political cultures of her time, her career is

deserving of full attention and analysis by scholars of literature and history.

       Edmonia Lewis was born in 1844 just outside of Albany, New York. Her

parents passed away when she was very young. She lived with her mother’s family,

who were Native American, until her brother was able to pay for her formal education.

After living in Central New York and Northwest Ohio, where she attended school, she

lived in Boston, Massachusetts, where she was able to establish herself as an artist.

During her time in the Massachusetts, she sold artwork, taught and saved the money

she needed to relocate to Rome, Italy, where she would continue her

professionalization. Lewis would maintain residence in Europe for the rest of her life,

but as a woman who loved to travel, she often made transatlantic journeys, to build her
career, and to create connections in both the United States and Europe. She passed

away in 1907, in London, England.
