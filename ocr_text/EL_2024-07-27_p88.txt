       Lewis implied that she too was on the receiving end of this government led

assimilation project for Native Americans. She describes it thus: “I went to school first

in Albany. I was delighted to learn--very eager. I had never learned anything. The

‘black robes’ taught us a few prayers--that was all.”161 This suggests that, prior to

attending New York Central College, the name of the school in Albany to which she

referred, Lewis was exposed to nuns, or “black robes” who provided religious

education and, as suggested using the word “us,” other indigenous people. Henry and
