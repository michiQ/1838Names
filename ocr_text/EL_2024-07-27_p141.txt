       These two gatherings are important examples of African American patronizing

Lewis. Her white patrons were generally able to purchase sculptures in lumpsum from

their own resources. African Americans supported her collectively, through

subscription, through gifts such as the purse of money and the ivory mallet. In

addition, Forever Free was purchased on subscription before Lewis presented it to

Reverend Grimes. Since she arrived in America that summer, it was African American

affiliated and institutions and networks, including the Freedmen's Bureau, the AME

Church, and a myriad of interconnected individuals, who supported Lewis financially

and emotionally. African American churches were significant gathering places where

cultural production took place. Also, the African American church was hospitable to
