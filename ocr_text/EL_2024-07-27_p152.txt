                                    Introduction

       In 1874, African American historian and fiction writer William Wells Brown

published The Rising Son Or, The Antecedents and Advancement of the Colored

Race.296 His fifty-chapter history begins with the “antecedents” of African Americans

in ancient Africa, and ends with their “advancement,” demonstrated in the chapter

entitled, “Representative Men and Women.” This last chapter is a chronological

compendium of biographical sketches of African Americans who contributed to

African American cultural production in literature, the arts, and theology, amongst
