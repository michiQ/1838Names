       Outside of these texts, she is usually included in general art histories such as

Samella S. Lewis’s African American Art and Artists, published in 1990.12 These

publishing dates emphasize that Edmonia Lewis has only recently received book-

length attention, and that the few texts that have been published look at Lewis from

the perspective of popular biography and art history. Now, when scholars are just

starting to engage with Lewis as a significant historical figure, is the perfect time to

emphasize the extent to which she was embedded in a larger African American,

activist, literary community that crossed and re-crossed the Atlantic. My hope is that

this project will be one of many scholarly projects that use interdisciplinary methods

to bring life to Black archives and complexity to the historical figures we presume to

already know.

       That said, my project rests within the fields of Black archival studies and

historical and literary studies of late nineteenth-century Black women’s activism.13
