       In addition, the images from the carte-de-visite seem to serve as references for

the sketches of her that appear in newspapers. Throughout her career, Lewis was

intentional about her presence in the media. Over time, the tone, style, and content of

her interviews would change. These elements could be influenced by the interviewer,

the newspaper, her age, and the context in which she is being interviewed. However,

there are core elements that remain consistent. Generally, she is described as

intelligent, and interesting to talk to, and sometimes, there is a certain humor to her

responses that serve as an opportunity to explore the multivalence of her

communication style. Also consistent in her narrative are the details of her early

childhood and discovery of sculpture, and these details have a direct correlation to the

miniature book that Charles Sumner donated to Harvard.
