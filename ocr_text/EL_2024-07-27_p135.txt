        Sometime between June and July of 1869, Lewis made her way to Boston,

where she received a warm welcome. She attended the Festival of the Teachers of the

Freedmen’s Union Commission, which took place late in the afternoon on July 1st.

The National Anti-Slavery Standard, as well as an August 1st printing of the

Freedmen’s Record, describe the event. In the summer, educators from the north who

taught in the south would come home, making it an ideal time for a celebration of their

contributions.259 In the summer of 1865, shortly before she first left for Rome, Lewis

traveled from Boston to Richmond to teach under the Freedmen's Bureau, so it is

possible that she knew some of the teachers that were in attendance. There is also

another reason she might have been at this event. The writer describes the scene at the

Parker Fraternity Hall: “The gallery was hung with flags, busts of Sumner and Phillips

occupied prominent places on the walls, and bouquets were plentifully bestowed on

the tables, desk and piano.” In 1865, Lewis completed a bust of Wendell Phillips, so

perhaps the bust of Phillips at this event was hers. After the teachers were honored,

“similar attention was paid to William Lloyd Garrison, John G. Whittier, the Rev. J. T.
