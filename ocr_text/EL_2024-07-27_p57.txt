Leslie and Tuckerman were of the mind that with experience and learning, Lewis

would graduate from sentimental, girlish themes to more ‘serious’ undertakings. Even

so, they gave her the responsibility for Native subjects, as if she could be the token

representative for works of this kind. This letter was quoted in smaller publications

across the nation several years after 1867. By printing and reprinting this commentary,

Leslie and Tuckerman reified the demand for a Native sculptor to create Native works.

Although this perspective limited Lewis, she found ways to manipulate it, and to turn

it into her own story using a sort of inventiveness that capitalized on the public’s

demand.

Child. It seemed as if she had plans to work on her own marble of the famous native

princess. In a July 1868 letter to Harriet Sewall, Child wrote the following regarding

she had been used to seeing young Indian girls, and could work from memory, and
