In fact, several Oberlin community members were connected to major events leading

up to the Civil War. Owen Brown, one of Oberlin’s trustees from 1835-1844, was an

outspoken abolitionist. His son, John Brown, the subject of one of Lewis’ most

famous portrait busts, would become a national hero for leading the raid on Harpers

Ferry, which also took place the year Lewis began at Oberlin. Additionally, three of

those who resisted at Harper’s Ferry were African Americans from the town of

Oberlin. Mary Jane Patterson’s husband, Lewis Sheridan Leary, was killed during the

raid.194 By the end of her time at Oberlin, Ohio was third in Union Army recruits after
