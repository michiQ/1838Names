boundaries of the patriarchal home structure, are marginal figures that occupy liminal

spaces within the domestic space. How then, would an Afro-Indian orphan girl be

read?

        Within the gender-racial hierarchy, non-white women are not considered to be

ladies worthy of protection, making them virtually unrapable.139 However, they are

not considered to be men either, as historically, Black women have been revoked of

their reproductive rights and they have often been forced into the role of caretakers,

occupying a very intimate space within white homes. I would argue that in this

hierarchy, non-white orphan girls occupy a gender-queer space, or a space whose

liminality is caused by intersecting factors: their parent-lessness, and their non-
