representative of the hopes for racial equality that many abolitionists espoused.

Edmonia Lewis gained notoriety with each new commission and exhibition in the

United States. By the time Sumner made his donation, news had spread that the

European royal Marquis de Bute was one of Edmonia Lewis’ newest patrons.59

Additionally, her sculpture of Hagar, which she exhibited at Farwell Hall in Chicago,

attracted a lot of attention. As an advocate of abolition and equal rights, Sumner would

have been a supporter of Lewis’ success.
