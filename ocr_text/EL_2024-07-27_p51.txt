       While she changed some of the details of her childhood depending on the time

and place of the interview, Lewis’ narrative is consistent in that she positions herself

as having been heavily influenced by her mother’s family in her formative years. In

her first interview with Lydia Maria Child, in 1864, Lewis described her life with her

mother’s family as follows: “I did as my mother's people did. I made baskets and

embroidered moccasins, and I went into the cities with my mother's people to sell

them.”79 In the summer of 1873, by which point she had gained international attention,

she visited the Daily Graphic in New York City, where she gave an interview. She

said that after the death of her parents, “My Indian aunts took care of me; the tribe

moved away to Canada; but they sometimes came down as far as New York City to

buy beads and such things.”80 In another interview in Indianapolis that same year, she
