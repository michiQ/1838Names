Bay’s 2021 book, Traveling Black: A Story of Race and Resistance offers some

helpful insight that gives us a sense of what Lewis might have experienced.252 In the

first chapter of her book, titled, “Road to Plessy: How Travel Segregation Took

Shape,” Bay provides a well-supported description of the injustice and insult Black

travelers faced while using public transportation throughout the nineteenth century.

She relies on personal testimony from Black newspapers, court decisions, and other

forms of print material to give readers a sense of the experience of Black travelers and

the efforts they made to seek justice. Bay frames the chapter with anecdotes from an

unpublished essay by Mary Church Terrell, starting the chapter with an experience

Church Terrell had when she was a young child in the 1860s traveling with her father,

and ending it with an experience she had while traveling with her daughters in the

1890s. This is the approximate time frame that Lewis was active and in the public eye,

so it serves as a useful point of reference for thinking about what her experience might

have been. Bay also quotes Church Terell’s memories of traveling alone at night from
