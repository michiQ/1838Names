         How Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist in Black Literary History

       One of the driving questions of this chapter is, how does How Edmonia Lewis

Became an Artist contribute to our understanding of nineteenth-century black

women’s literature? When read alongside her interviews, Edmonia Lewis’ How

Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist influenced how she was remembered by Black

writers and historians. The exclamation in her narrative, “Oh, how I should like to

make something like that man standing there!” had a profound influence on how her

story was told in the media and other sorts of printed works. In an 1893 anthology of

biographical sketches of accomplished Black women, titled Women of Distinction, for

example, Lawson A. Scruggs seems to pull directly from this pamphlet.150 William

Wells Brown’s 1874 history, The Rising Son Or, The Antecedents and Advancement of

the Colored Race pulls directly from Laura Curtis Bullard’s interview with Edmonia

Lewis, published in her magazine, the Revolution. Bullard’s article takes its inspiration

from How Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist as well. Monroe Alphus Majors’

biography of Edmonia Lewis, included in his anthology Noted Negro Women, cites a
