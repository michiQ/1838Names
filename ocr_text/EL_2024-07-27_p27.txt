       First, the way we characterize historical figures matters. One of my goals was

to find ways to discuss Lewis as autonomous, intelligent, and cognizant of her social

and political contexts. Her pamphlet, How Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist, gave me

an opportunity to differentiate between the writing that shapes her public persona and

what her decisions reveal about her personality. An undated text with no clear author,

it is possible that How Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist was written by Lewis herself.

Even if Lewis did not write this pamphlet, her interviews corroborate its contents, and

provided a great opportunity to write about print and public identity. Chapter two,

“Edmonia Lewis’ Engagement with Her Public: A Close Reading of How Edmonia
Lewis Became an Artist,” places Lewis within literary culture. I offer a close reading

direct role in shaping her public persona by distributing this pamphlet. I identify the
