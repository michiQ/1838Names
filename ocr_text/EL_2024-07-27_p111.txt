September of 1841, Frederick Douglass and James N. Buffum knowingly entered the

first-class car of a train at the Lynn, Massachusetts station. The conductor ordered

them to leave the car. When they refused, two brakemen tried to forcibly remove

them, resulting in a fight. By this point, Douglass was well known, yet his social

position was not a buffer against such treatment.212

        The African Americans of Massachusetts were not allowed to serve in the

Army until the establishment of the 54th Regiment during the Civil War. This gives

added significance to Lewis’ desire to sculpt the regiment’s leader, Colonel Shaw.213

events and gatherings. Sarah Parker Remond, who also lived in Rome and would come
