                                      Introduction

       The Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative began with the Indian

Civilization Act of March 3, 1819, passed to encourage the “civilization process” of

native peoples. This act initiated a cultural genocide that lasted through the 1970s.158

Thousands of native children were forcibly removed from their homes and placed in

boarding schools. The purpose was to, as Carlisle Indian Industrial School

superintendent Captain Richard Henry Pratt put it in his 1892 speech, “Kill the Indian
