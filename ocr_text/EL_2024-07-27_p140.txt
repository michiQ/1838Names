       By fall of 1869, Lewis was back in Boston. On October 18th, a Monday

evening, Lewis presented her marble group, “Forever Free,” to Reverend Leonard

Grimes. Like Reverend Loguen, Reverend Grimes was pivotal to the Underground

Railroad, and assisted many freedom seekers. His church, Twelfth Baptist, was located

on Phillips Street. Known as the “The Fugitive Slave Church,” many who escaped

slavery chose to join Twelfth Baptist’s congregation. Leonard Grimes would remain

minister of the church until his death in 1874.271 The occasion was a joyous one, with

music by an unnamed African American woman and speeches by William Lloyd

Garrison, Rev J. D. Fulton and R. C. Waterston and William Craft.272 A few weeks

later, in November, Edmonia Lewis’ friends gathered once more, this time at the
