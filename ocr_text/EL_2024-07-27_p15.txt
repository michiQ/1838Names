  Project Overview: Edmonia Lewis in the Context of Post-Bellum, Pre-Harlem
                                 America

       In this project, I situate Lewis in the context of Black women’s political and

literary networks of the late nineteenth century. My dissertation identifies Lewis as an

artist whose sculptures, while drawing on neoclassical aesthetics, responded directly to

this lineage of Black activism. I also situate her as a public figure who carefully

curated her persona as a contributor to African American cultural production. My

study demonstrates that Lewis' experience of living in multiple countries did not

preclude her participation in African American discourse and culture. Like her

contemporaries (most of whom she met), including Sarah Parker Remond, Henry

Highland Garnet, Henry Ossawa Tanner, William and Ellen Craft, and even Frederick

Douglass, Lewis utilized her access to a larger Black Atlantic network to liberate
