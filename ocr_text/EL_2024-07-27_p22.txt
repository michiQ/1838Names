reader, and writer, and as a person who responded to literature through art, Edmonia

Lewis was a part of the literary community of this period. Placing Edmonia Lewis in a

larger Black literary community shows the extent to which African Americans valued

developing a robust cultural identity, one that was involved in all forms of the arts and

humanities, and one that would respond to racist discourse in a variety of ways, that

extend beyond the written and spoken and into the visual. In addition, Lewis’ identity

as a biracial woman connected to the African American community points to the

diversity of what it means to be African American. Her involvement in and

recognition by this community reveals that African Americans sought to achieve on all

fronts, an aspect of freedom, and that cultural development was a part of the tenants of

freedom for them.
