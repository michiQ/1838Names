           Becoming an Artist: The Americanization of Edmonia Lewis

       How Edmonia Lewis Became an Artist relies on the wide appeal of the

Bildungsroman as a narrative structure. Lewis and her brother Samuel’s development

in the narrative reads as a process of Americanization and maturation from ‘wild’ and

‘uncivilized’ orphans, to educated Christian adults who follow a higher calling, a

change that would appeal to many white, and some nonwhite readers. Midway through

the first paragraph of the narrative, the reader learns that Lewis was orphaned at an

early age. The author introduces her brother Samuel shortly after:

       Her brother, who was at a school for Indian boys at the time of his
       mother's death, soon finished his studies and went to California, where
       he was able in a few years to send money to his little orphan sister, that
       she might be sent to school.112
