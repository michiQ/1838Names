Lewis made a drawing of Urania, the Greek muse of astronomy, for a friend as a

wedding present.318 Given the skill exhibited in this drawing, it is likely that she had

been drawing for some time. First year students in the “Ladies Course” were offered

one complementary linear drawing course, and additional courses were available, for a

fee. Lewis’ biographers Henderson and Henderson state that Lewis also made a bust

of an unidentified professor out of putty around this time.319 Perhaps, during their

meeting, Lewis showed Douglass some other pieces she had created. Perhaps Lewis

told Douglass what transpired between her and this art teacher who accused her of

stealing supplies. These early signs of talent make the circumstances around her

expulsion from Oberlin even more egregious. Second, a few years earlier, Douglass

joined Sarah Parker Remond (who would later move to Rome as well), the sister of his

good friend Charles Lenox Remond, on a speaking tour through England, Ireland, and

Scotland. He would have been able to share his travel experience with Lewis. Third,

Lewis’ brother Samuel traveled through Europe in 1862. This would set the precedent

for Lewis to feel open to making the transition to Europe.
