Her pamphlet included an essay by I. Garland Penn called “The Art of Sculpture.” The

essay includes a brief sketch of Edmonia Lewis and her most famous works, but,

interestingly enough, does not mention “Death of Cleopatra.”351 Wells was not the

only one who tried to advocate for African American representation at the World’s

Fair. In reference to his own efforts to demand Black representation at the World’s

Fair, Turner wrote the following in “To Colored People”:

       I went three times in person and used every argument at my command
       and scores of others did the same, but no recognition was given the
       negro whatever, while $30,000,000 was expended, much of which
       consisted of taxes paid by the colored race. The magnificent painting
       by Professor H. O. Tanner, which was upon exhibition there, was
       painted in Paris, France, and sent to this country as a part of the French
       exhibits, and the American negro is entitled to no credit for the
       acceptance of that piece of fine art nor the premium that was given it.352
