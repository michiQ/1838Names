Florence, written on May 20th of 1867 confirms: “She has read ‘Evangeline,’ and

some others of Longfellow's poems, and has caught from them a girlish sentimentality,

by Frank Leslie on July 20th of 1867 in his widely circulating New York publication,

Frank Leslie’s Illustrated Newspaper.91

       Henry T. Tuckerman, a leading American art historian, travel writer, and critic,

excerpted Leslie’s profile on Lewis for his chapter focused on sculptors in his six-

hundred-page survey entitled Book of Artists, that was published in 1867. After his

commentary on Lewis’ Hiawatha works, he offers this recommendation, or request:

       By and by, when her horizon of knowledge becomes more expanded,
       and her grasp on it firmer, she will leave the prettinesses of poems, and
       give us Pocahontas, Logan, Pontiac, Tecumseh, Red Jacket, and, it may
       be, Black Hawk and Osceola. Or if these may seem too near and real,
       and admitting less of effective accessories, there lie behind them all the
       great dramatic characters, Montezuma, Guatimozin, Huascar, and
       Atahualpa, to say nothing of the Malinche, that lost her country that she
       might save her love.92
