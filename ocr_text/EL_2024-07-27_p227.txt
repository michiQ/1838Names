                                       Appendix D

             HENRY WREFORD, ANTHENAEUM, MARCH 3, 1866


       Miss Edmonia Lewis has a very engaging appearance and manners. Her eyes

and the upper part of her face are fine; the crisp hair and thick lips, on the other hand,

bespeak her negro paternity. Naïve in manner, happy and cheerful, and all-

unconscious of difficulty, because obeying a great impulse, she prattles like a child,

and with much simplicity and spirit pours forth all her aspirations.376




376 Henry Wreford, "A Negro Sculptress." The Athenaeum, no. 2001, March 3, 1866,
302, ProQuest.
