lifetime influence how she has been remembered across different eras within the Black

Freedom Struggle? While most of the scholarship on Lewis comes from art history,

the methodologies I will use to complete this project will rely on, and add to, the body

of archival recovery work led by Black woman scholars in literary and African

American studies, as well as the body of work on Black print culture specifically.

Hopefully, the interdisciplinary nature of this project will inspire more creative

approaches to the study of art history and literary studies.

       This project will add dimension to how we understand Black women’s

activism and Black women’s values during this time. Furthermore, it will mark what

Barbara McCaskill calls the post-Bellum, pre-Harlem period as an era during which

the seeds were sown for the full blossoming of the Harlem Renaissance. While the

Harlem Renaissance is noted as the beginning of what would be an explosion of Black
arts in all genres throughout the twentieth century, connecting Lewis to a larger
