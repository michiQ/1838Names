             To Be Black in 1860s Boston: Edmonia Lewis in Context

       Boston could be called the “cultural center of New England.” While the city

was a hub for abolitionist activism, and had always been a center for political

resistance, Boston had its fair share of racial tension and inequality.211 There had been

decades of protest segregation in public spaces. Boston had been a center for

resistance against the slave act, yet segregation was common in public spaces.

and the militia. Boston had one of the most politically active Black antebellum

communities in America and it remained so after the Civil War. While the sit-in is a
