       Third, Lewis was a world traveler who had affective experiences and

connections in America. This realization shapes chapters four and five. Chapter four,

“Around Half the World in Thirty Days: Edmonia Lewis navigates the Black

Atlantic,” is a meditation on Lewis’ experience as a Black woman traveler. The first

chapter of Mia Bay’s 2021 book, Traveling Black: A Story of Race and Resistance

provided a needed backdrop for thinking about Lewis’ experience as a travel. 27 I

locate Lewis at the intersection of race, class, gender, and travel, placing emphasis on

the physical, emotional, and financial risks she faced as a non-white woman who

traveled with fragile sculptures across the Atlantic. I bring attention to a dimension of

nineteenth-century culture that deserves more attention: Black networks of care

provided shelter, labor, and spaces of physical and psychological safety during Lewis’

visits to the United States. Indeed, hospitality provided well-needed support for Lewis

as she made her entrepreneurial endeavors.
