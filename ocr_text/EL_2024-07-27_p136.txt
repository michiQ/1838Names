       In mid-July, several papers reprinted the subscription fundraiser for Lewis’

Longfellow. Lewis might have used the Teacher’s Festival as an opportunity to share

her work amongst friends. According to The Freedmen’s Record, “Miss Edmonia

Lewis, the gifted young sculptor of mixed African and Indian blood, who has just

returned from Italy, was also present, and received a bouquet and a warm

welcome.”262 For William Lloyd Garrison, one of her first Boston supporters, this

would have been his first time seeing her in almost five years. There were likely other

people in attendance that Lewis knew or hadn’t seen in some time, and even more

likely is that many were inquiring about her progress. Assumably, there were some in

attendance who knew that she was struggling financially--a difficulty that had been

published in newspapers across the country prior to her arrival. It is also likely that she

had photographs of her works on hand so that she would share them with anyone who
