New Era, and soon after renamed it, The New National Era. This paper covered the

progress of Reconstruction and highlighted issues concerning African Americans.

Douglass was prompted to write “Miss Edmonia Lewis” in response to the media

attention Lewis received during her 1873 visit. She was causing a great stir because

she spoke very freely and openly about the scandal around Vinnie Ream, her

contemporary accused of being commissioned by congress to do a bust of Lincoln

because of her beauty, rather than her talent. Some believed that her assistants were

behind the making of her sculpture.

       In her interviews with the Daily News and the Sun, Lewis placed emphasis on

the friends and allies she made throughout her career. Douglass acknowledged this in

his article, referencing the “advantages” Lewis has enjoyed. However, he also

mentioned the challenges she faced, without making a direct reference to a particular

incident. His statement reads as follows:

       We are aware that, in Italy Miss Lewis has enjoyed some advantages on
       account of the accident of complexion; has met encouragement where
       she would not had she been white. We know that this was the case in
       Boston; but we are still painfully aware that she has suffered many
       discouragements at the hands of her own fellow countrymen and
       women, which have tended to cripple her energies and clip the wings of
       her progress.317
