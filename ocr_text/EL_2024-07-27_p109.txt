       Most biographical sketches of Lewis note that Garrison introduced Lewis to

Edward Brackett, who provided her early training in sculpture. What is not noted

enough is the role Garrison played in introducing her to a literary community. Lewis

pledged $5.00 ($100 in 2024) to the Liberator in 1864 at the New England Anti-

Slavery Convention in May.207 She supported the mission and the work that the

Liberator was doing, and likely was grateful for the support she had received from

Garrison and the Liberator. By that point, her January 29 article publicizing her John

Brown Medallion had been republished seven times.208 She was also listed in the

Thirtieth National Anti-Slavery Subscription Anniversary List on January 27, 1864,

with a donation amount of $1.00.209 In an article describing the Anniversary

Celebration on the same page, Lydia Maria Child described her first encounter with

Lewis. She was mentioned among John G. Whittier, Theodore G Weld, and Wendell

Phillips; however, most of the letter focused on Lewis and mentioned the John Brown

Medallion. Since Lewis was that significant to Lydia Maria Child, was she also as

remarkable to these other men? Did she stay for the entirety of the reception, and if so,

did she stay to listen to these individuals? Since she was a subscriber, how often was

she reading and what essays were catching her attention? Phillips was an abolitionist
