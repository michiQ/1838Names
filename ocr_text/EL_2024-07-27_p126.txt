this route dramatically improved. With improved coordination between east and west,

and the construction of the railroad connecting the east and west coast of Panama,

travel decreased from 35 days to 21 days.242 The first transcontinental railroad was

completed in 1869; however, there were bodies of water that travelers had to cross by

ferry.243 The 2,000-mile railroad connecting Iowa, Nebraska and California took only

4 days to commute. As of March of 1872, it was possible to travel across the United

States entirely by railway due to the construction of the Union Pacific Missouri River

Bridge.244 These changes would make both shipping and transportation easier,

creating the circumstances for the exchange of both vital commodities and cultural

products, from pianos, to of course, large sculptures.
