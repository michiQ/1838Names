practice during these visits were essential to the progress of her career and to

nineteenth century African American culture. Much like other American born

residents of Europe such as Ira Aldridge, the Remond family, Henry “Box” Brown,”

William and Ellen Craft, and Henry O. Tanner, or travelers such as William Wells

Brown, Frederick Douglass, James McCune Smith, Edmonia Lewis should be placed

in the context of the Black Atlantic. She was part of a small group of nineteenth

century African Americans who strove for their version of the American dream

outside of the borders of the United States nation-state, while retaining social and

cultural ties to their place of birth.
