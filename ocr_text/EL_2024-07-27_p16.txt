       I analyze the archive of the pro-Black press (here defined as Black owned

newspapers as well as abolitionist newspapers, and later white-owned newspapers that

support African American equality) as it relates to Edmonia Lewis to examine the

interaction between Black literary and visual cultures. I am particularly interested in

the intersections between the Woman’s Era (as the 1890s came to be known, based on

the title of the first Black women’s newspaper), the politics of respectability, and the

circumstances leading to the Harlem Renaissance and the New Negro period.5 It is

with these historical markers that I tell the story of Edmonia Lewis. The goal of this

project will be to reveal the presence of a nineteenth century, international, women-led
