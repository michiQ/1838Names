   The bulk of writing on nineteenth century women travelers is focused on the
experiences of white women, not only because white people made up most
independent and leisure travelers, but also because of the amount of travel writing that

More recently, Emma Robinson-Tomsett’s 2013 essay “No Nice Girl Swears: Advice,
Etiquette and Expectation,” appeared in Women, Travel and Identity: Journeys by Rail
and Sea, 1870-1940. These essays reveal the hegemonic gender constructs that may
have influenced how Black women, including Lewis, comported themselves, but as

these essays do not highlight the pressures and risks that influence the experiences of
Black women like Lewis who traveled alone in America during the 1860s and 1870s.
