Black churches, which I will describe next. The concept of the American museum

would still be in its nascent stage by the later stage of Lewis’ career, but African

American churches and colleges were the first homes for African American art, and

the significance of Black churches as art spaces and community spaces comes into

clearer view when we look at Lewis’ visits to the United States.


          San Francisco: A New Frontier for Black Community Building

       By the time Edmonia Lewis would take her trip to San Francisco, sleeping cars

had existed for some thirty years, but with the founding of the Pullman Palace Car

Company in 1867, the comfort and convenience of transcontinental travel improved--

for the uppermost classes. A first-class ticket in a Pullman sleeping car was $136,

second class was $110, and a third-class bench was $65.275 It's possible that Edmonia

could afford a first-class ticket, yet it is unlikely that purchasing one would have

permitted her to enter the first-class cars. While there would not be clear legislation

requiring racial segregation on trains until the late nineteenth century, de facto

segregation and the threat of violence was baked into the experience of travel for

Black people, especially those who demanded the same accommodations as white

passengers. From the earliest days of railroad travel, there were reports of Black

women being thrown off both moving and stationary trains for demanding equal

accommodation. This ongoing abuse culminated with the first Supreme Court case
