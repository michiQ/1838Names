       Lewis was a cultural interlocutor, bridging several aspects of Black cultural

practice and tradition to create an altogether new experience. Her presence disrupted

the gender biases of her day; she was the focal point of a commemorative practice that

took place in a public space where men were normally in leadership, presenting a form

of art traditionally created by (white) men and adapting it to the cultural and political

prerogatives of the African American community. For this reason, she belongs to a

lineage of Black women who participated in public culture, as discussed by Rebecca

Fraser, Martha Jones, Bettye Collier-Thomas, Denise Burgher, Victoria W. Wolcott,
