What is the influence of our daily press ?—
Many of our public papers are curses-instead

tents? Isnot Vice called Virtue; and is not
every. exertion made—every artifice used to
trap the unsuspecting and inexperienced pert
of mankind 7—T hese papers are so cheap and

{amilies—they are to be seen in every village,

im tts plessed land of light and liberty.

Whatever either a good or bad mind wish-
es to, be circulated, immediately goes forth to
be read by every eye that may see it, and to
bless or to pullute every heart that may feel it.

- In this state of things, mere ability to read

-lowing of all that is or is not of healthy di-
gestion, might also be questioned as to being
areal benefit to.any one. There must be
something more, or there is no safety. There

‘| must be a capacity anda relish for solid men-

tal nutriment. There must bea taste for un-
adulterated truth, ond a power to separate it,
whenever presented, from the mass of error
and sophistry which so frequently appear,
claiming its name and wearing its dress.

The mind, in order to do this, must be un-
ideradiscipline ; and yet it is a discipline at-
tainable by all; as all have, or may have,
the necessary means, and can successfully use
them. By this discipline is intended the
rability to fix the attention on a given subject,
frill itis understood in some or in all of its
various relations, and then to select the
_ thoughts which really belong to from those
, which do not, and then.to arrange the appyo-
‘ priate ones by themselves and to dismiss the
| inappropriate ones to the winds.
| Hewho can go through this process, and
every person can, by suitable care and reflect-
ion, and by that share of intelligence within
E reach, will be able easily to seperate the

 

wheat from the chaff, the precious from the
vile, in the many books, pamphlets, and news-
papers which may fall in his way. - But he
who will not go through this process, may be
either benefited or injured, by what he reads.
His benefit will be altogether accidental; and
his injury may be unsuspected and unseen,
till it is perhaps incurable in his mind. He
is exposed to temptation without the power
of resistance.
