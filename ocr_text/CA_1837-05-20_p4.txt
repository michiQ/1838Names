IMMORTALITY.

The prospect of a future state is
comfort and refreshment of my soul 3 it is
that which makes nature look gay about me 5
itdoubles all my pleases, and supports me
under all my afflictions. 1 can look at disap-
pointmentand inisfortunes, pain and sickness,
and death itself, and, what is worse than
death, the loss of those who are dearest to me,
with indifference, so long as I keep in view
the pleasures of eternity, and the state of be-
ing in which there will be no fears or appre-
hensions, pains, or sorrows, sickness or sep-
aration, Why will any man be so imperti-
nent as tu tell me all this is only fancy and
delusion 2?) Is there any merit in being the
messenger of il news? Wit isa dream, let
me enjoy it, since it inakes me both the hap-
pier and the better man.

EVIL SPEAKING,

They that will observe nothing in a wise
man, but his oversights and follies; nothing
in a good, but his failings and infirmities,
inay render both despicable. Should we heap
together all the passionate specehes, all the
imprudent actions, of the best man, and pre-
sent them all at one view, concealing his wis-
dom and virtues; he, in this disguise, would
look like a madinan or a fury.
his life were fairly represented in the manner
it was led, he would appear to all the world
to be an admirable and excellent person.—
But how numerous soever any man’s ill qual-
ities are, it is just that he should have the due
praise of his few real virtues.

CHEERFULNESS.

A woman may be of great assistance to
her husband, in business, by wearing a cheer-
ful smile continually upon her countenance.
A man’s perplexities and gloominess are in-

creased a hundred fold when his better half

moves about witha continual scowl upon her
brow, A pleasant cheerful wife is as a rain-
bow sct in the-sky when her husband’s mind
is tossed with storms and tempests 3 but.a dis-
satisfied and fretful wife, in the hour of trou-
ble, is like one of those fiends who are ap-
pointed to torture lost spirits,
