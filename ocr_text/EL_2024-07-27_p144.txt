perseverance a thorough knowledge of the fine and tasteful art of Sculpture to a degree

of perfection seldom reached.” The article concludes with the statement, “We advise

all our readers and everybody else to go.”278 Based on how the Pacific Appeal staff

describe Lewis, it is likely that the praise they give her in their August 30th article

came out of a desire to endorse her to influence their readers. If Lewis herself scripted

parts of the article to drum up interest in her exhibition, then there is a case to be made

for the power of Lewis’ self-advocacy. What is likely the case is that Lewis' business

savvy and confidence, in combination with the reputation preceding her, helped make

the task of marketing her work that much easier.
