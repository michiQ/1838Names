Brother Cornish,—

I hope you will gratify me so much as to pub-
lish, in connection with this, the extract alluding
tu your paper. Itis only one, out of the many, I
have met with in the course of my readings. tis
from the Philanthropist, an able paper, published
at Cinemnati, Ohio. The perusal of this and -i-
milar © Opinions of the Public Press,” have now
completely confirmed me in the belief of what I
have so often stated to you, in private conversati-
on:—" That the permanent establishment of a
FREE PRESS, among our colored brethren,
well conducted, entirely under their own supervi-
sion, will have a most powerful tendency in has-
tening the termination of that system of oppressi-
on, Which, [ beheve, had its origin in the infernal
regions, and is only supported at the present day,
by some “choice characters,’ whose spirits and
principles emanate from that quarter.”

Yes, Sir, I verily believe, that itis much easier
to raise the voice of compassion and justice from
our white oppressors, by an open and frank avow-
al of our grievances, emanating directly from our-
selves; fur we thereby bring them into collision
with the most irresistible arguments, proving be-
yond all doubt, that colored people are MEN, and
as such, fully entitled to all Freedom, political and
religious, to its full extent, equal with the white
man.

Lain glad to hear that your tittle gazette is doing
good, among those for whose special benclitit wis
intended. GOD is at work. Qur people shall
LIVE. We are ONE with the Abolitionists. Our
principles are the same. And we are all,whatever
may be our color, the children of one gracious
Heavenly Father. &. S.
