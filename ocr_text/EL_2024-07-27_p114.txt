       In the census, Addie (Ada), who was around the same age as Edmonia, was

listed as a teacher, and Edmonia herself was listed as a sculptor.218 While Ada was a

child during much of Black Boston’s protest of segregated school, having grown up in

Massachusetts would have given her enough perspective to value the education of

African American children. In addition, teaching was the best profession for African

American women who sought an opportunity outside of domestic labor. So, Ada

dedicated her life to the vocation. She and Edmonia were employed under the

Freedman’s Bureau in the summer of 1865 to teach in Richmond, Virginia.219 Ada

died in 1922 at age 77 in Philadelphia as a single, retired schoolteacher.220 It would be

nice to think that she and Lewis formed a meaningful friendship.
