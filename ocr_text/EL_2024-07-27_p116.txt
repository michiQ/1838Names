Lewis’ friendship with the Howard sisters shows us a side of her that can change how

we understand her politics as biracial and woman. The sisters were two Black women

that were dedicated to education and, in Joan Imogene’s case, Black cultural

production and representation. What sort of conversations did Edmonia Lewis have

with the sisters during their younger years? Perhaps it was their conversations that

convinced Lewis to apply to teach in Richmond with the Freedman’s Bureau in the

first place. We know that she was in Richmond for at least one month, and likely

longer.225 This temporary position was framed as an opportunity to make money for

her transition to Rome. However, Lewis should be given more credit for deciding to

contribute to the cause of education for African Americans, even if it was not her

long-term career choice. Indeed, Buick points out that Lewis would have made around

twenty dollars after one month of teaching, asserting that she did not go for money,

but rather, a sense of “civic duty.”226 If we give more meaning to the affective
experience she might have had while in Richmond with Ada Howard, we can ask

additional questions: Even though her teaching experience was brief, how does it

change our perspective if we emphasize the fact that she was deemed eligible to do so?
