                                     Appendix G

EXCERPT FROM “A COLORED SCULPTRESS,” APRIL 14, 1869


       For a girl so young, and without friends or patrons, it is a notable example of

courage and self-reliance. And it is the more so because, as perhaps I should have

mentioned on the start, she is u negro girl-or rather, is of mixed Indian and negro

blood, the latter quite predominating. Her name is Edmonia Lewis; her father was a

Chippewa, and her mother was Black. I think neither of them are living. When I

called, she was in her working dress-an overfrock of tow cloth-and busy with the

chisel; but she received me with a very pleasing and natural grace, with an absence of

any affectation which was quite charming. 379




379 “A Colored Sculptress,” Milwaukee Daily Sentinel, April 14, 1869, Nineteenth
Century U.S. Newspapers.
