CONDUCT OF THE MINISTERS
AND CHUKCHES.

The conduct of a large majority of the
clergy, together with that of the churches un-
der thetr contrul, during the past year, has
been such as to deepen the conviction, long
since forced upon reflecting and conscientious
tninds, that they constitute the main pillar
which supports the bloody and polluted, tho’
tottering temple of oppression! The inge-
nious defence of slavery from the Bible, by
learned Doctors of Divinity, who exert a con-
trolling influence upon the public mind, and
whose opinions regulate the standard of mo-
rality in respect to popular sins, have done
more than all other causes combined, to sooth
the consciences of hardened transgressors, and
lull the nation to sleep over the fearful gulph
into which it seems to be rhadly rushing.—
We have spoke with indignation of reere-
ant statesmen, and corrupt and time-serving
politicians; we have denounced as treacher-
ous to the fundamental principles of liberty
those members of Congress who have dared
to trample on the sacred right of petition, and
the conduct of the President of the United
States, in pledging himself to becomea tool
to perpetuate the horrors of slavery, has exci-
ted our just abhorrence ; but the guilt of such
is brighter than Gossamer in comparison with
the pondecous criminality of those who

occupy elevated stations in the Church, we
must clothe our thoughts in soft and gentle
language. But experience has proved the
folly of such a course. If we are to assume
a mild and conciliating aspect towards any
class of men, it should be, not towards these,
but to the unenlightened and confiding per-
sons, whom they have deluded by their so-
phistry, and led away by their wicked exam-
ple. Christ was full of compassion for the

will feel the power of our rebukes, and be led
