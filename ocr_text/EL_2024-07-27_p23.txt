Moreover, Daina Ramey Berry and Kali Nicole Gross--who highlight Black women’s

slavery--add to our understanding of Black women’s travel across the Atlantic.18 Mia

Bay and Elizabeth Stordeur Pryor’s comprehensive study of Black travel provides

context for Edmonia Lewis’s experience of traveling across the continental United

States throughout her career. Telling the story of Lewis as a woman who received

American news coverage while she was living across the Atlantic will add to the

contention that the Black Atlantic existed for far longer than often assumed by

academics. Lewis’ story, and the way that story was chronicled in the press, aligns

with what Gilroy calls the “unthinking assumption that cultures always flow into

patterns congruent with the borders of essentially homogeneous nation states.”19
