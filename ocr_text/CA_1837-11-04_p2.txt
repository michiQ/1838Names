WE have read nothing, for a long time, that
pleased us so much as Mr. Wattles’ Letter upon
the 4th page of our paper hst week. Our breth-
ren of Ohio and Indiana have adopted the true
policy. They have found the philosopher's
stone, and in spite of their enemies and traducers,
will elevate themselves in the scale of being, and

lay a Sunndation for the prosperity and happiness spiration,we find the true standard of noxy Living

of their children, as lasting as the Western hills,
or as the fertility of the Western vallies.

Let colored men, if they would be men in Am-
erica, embody themselves as the producers and
mechanics of the country. This is the kind of
identity we need with our native home. This
will honorably connect us with the prosperity,and
make us participators in the glories of the Ameri-
can Republic.

What is more honorable than husbandry 7—
To till the soil, to be a producer of corn and
wheat, and a grower of flocks and herds, has
something in it paradisiacal. If there be any calling
in this world, allied to primitive innocency, it is
husbandry. ‘There is more independence of
circumstances—more safety from the common ca-
lamities of life in the business ofa farmer, than in
any other occupation.

We have often lamented the apparent aversion
to farming, among our brethren, This is decided-
ly the worst feature in our domestic character.—
Nothing else can possibly procure for us so much
independence and importance in this country, as
husbandry, and nothing is more easy. Any man
who can raise a few hundred doilars, may possess
himself of a good farm in the West, and procure
for himself and family, every comfort. Most of
the wealthy men of Ohio, Illinois, and the adjoin-
ing States, commenced with very lite capital.and
we have no doubt but that the brethren mentioned
in Br. Wattles’ Letter, will be very independent
in a few years.

Brother Wattles, we understand, intends visit-
ing the East, to confer with our brethren in the
older States, in the course of the coming winter
or spring. We shall hail his arrival with joy. If
he shall be so happy as to turn the attention of our
people to farming, we assure him as a benefactor
of the oppressed, our children shall rise up to call
hiun blessed.
