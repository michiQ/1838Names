Because she had large, fragile parcels with her, she was probably aware of the

following warning posted by the Cunard Line, a shipping company she booked with

several times throughout her career:

       Freight payable either here or in America at the current rate of

       received at the office of the agents here until six o'clock on the
       evenings previous to sailing.240

delivery of her sculptures, there was also the discoordination caused by the more than

140 local times that existed before the standardization of time zones in 1883.241 In the

months prior to her interview, Lewis shipped five sculptures to San Francisco for her

exhibition, adding another three thousand miles of transport along unsteady railways,
