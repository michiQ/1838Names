However, once she headed further west, to upstate New York and Ohio, racial

discrimination made it difficult for her to find accommodations. As the Buffalo

Commercial reported:

       Miss Edmonia Lewis, the colored sculptress, visited Cleveland last
       week, and going to the Kennard House, was given to understand that
       she could not be accommodated in that establishment. She then went to
       the Forest City House, where she was graciously taken in, but, ‘owing
       to prejudices existing in the public mind,’ was obliged to take her meals
       in the seclusion of her room.264

This was not the only time she would face this sort of humiliation. While some papers

made no mention of the insult she faced in Syracuse, the St. Louis Globe Democrat

provided more detail:

       It seems incredible that the curse of caste still rests upon our city, but it
       is none the less true that Miss Lewis was denied admittance to one of
       our hotels on account of her color. What a rebuke to free America, that
       accomplished and talented characters, whose fame is world-wide, are
       denied the commonest hospitality, while in despotic Europe they
       mingle in the circles of rank and fashion, honored and respected.265
