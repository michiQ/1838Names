       The Christian Recorder provided the most detailed information about

Edmonia’s Longfellow. They informed readers that, “A subscription has been started

to raise the required sum,” which was $700, or about $15,700 today. To validate the

quality of the bust, they quoted “Professor Childs” (most likely Harvard professor

James Francis Child) who said, “I have seen the photograph of Miss Lewis’ bust of

Professor Longfellow, and should consider a copy in marble a very desirable

acquisition for Harvard College. The college at present possesses no portrait, of any

sort, of Professor Longfellow.” Lewis also created an incentive. She proposed, “that if

any society will raise a hundred dollars towards this object, and will send the sum to

Fields, Osgood & Co., she will give them, for a present to their pastor, a copy of the

bust in terra cotta.”263 Aside from private commissions, churches, Black churches

were Lewis’ best audience for subscriptions and collective support.
